Artificial intelligence is no longer just a tool.

It is becoming a decision engine.

That shift matters because AI systems are starting to influence infrastructure, finance, operations, and human outcomes. But the outputs those systems produce are still probabilistic, which means a basic question remains unresolved:

How do we verify that an AI decision is actually correct?

That is the problem behind Zero Trust Intelligence (ZTI).

Zero Trust Intelligence (ZTI) is a protocol for verifiable AI systems. It introduces a deterministic verification layer between AI output and execution so that only verified decisions are allowed to pass.

Its core constraint is simple:
No decision is trusted unless it is proven.

In practice, ZTI transforms raw AI output through a fixed pipeline:
- Pattern Registry
- Detection
- Explainability
- Validation
- Integrity
- Lineage

If verification cannot be completed, the decision is rejected.

ZTI does not make AI smarter.
It makes AI accountable.
